about

Covert Coffee

Covert Coffee sits at 1643 Broadway in Bushwick, Brooklyn — a corner that's been serving the neighborhood since the 1880s. The building started life as H.C. Bohack's corner grocery in 1889, survived decades as everything from a flower market to a drug store, weathered the arson crisis of the 1970s, and sat vacant through the long quiet of the 1990s and 2000s.

In 2015, Aaron Mounier opened Covert behind a boarded-up storefront with a hand-lettered "coming soon" sign — and the name stuck. The shop roasts in small batches, pulls precise shots on a two-group La Marzocco, and serves sandwiches on locally baked bread. No Wi-Fi password on the wall, no merch rack by the door. Just good coffee in a building with 136 years of stories in its walls.
