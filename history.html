1888 – 1890s the building neighborhood

Built on a Farm

Chauncey Street and Halsey Street elevated train stations opened in the summer of 1885. Some time after, on what was once a farm owned by William Covert, an ornate four-story brick building was constructed at 1643 Broadway — which still stands today, housing Covert Coffee and its baristas.

Broadway was paved with cobblestone, and at Christmastime, prettily decorated with Yuletide green amongst attractive storefront displays. Streetcars were pulled along train tracks in the middle of the road by horses. A couple blocks up at Covert and Evergreen was Roth farm, where neighbors would fetch well-water for their houses and local kids would stash scrap wood for bonfires. Ol' Fink's Pond on Central Ave and Schaefer Street served the neighborhood as a swimming hole (bare) in Summer and skating pond in Winter.

"When all were in place we set fire to them. And was that a blaze — it even awakened Red, the cop, who tried to chase the entire neighborhood away from it. There were no buildings near enough in danger of getting afire."

— Brooklyn Daily Eagle Sun, Page 34

Near Myrtle Ave and Broadway kids would gather flowers in the Spring and Summer, and fruit from the orchards in the Fall. They would play baseball games in empty fields which were to become roads; they would build fires and roast spuds. They would steal rides on the train to Canarsie. Then one spring, Mr. Grassman began to build rows of houses where they had played and where cows were staked out to graze.

1889 – 1920s bohack's the building

Bohack's Corner Grocery

First of the businesses to utilize the corner retail space at 1643 Broadway was H.C. Bohack Co., Grocers. Henry Bohack immigrated to Brooklyn from a small seaport, Cuxhaven, Germany at the age of 17. He worked as a grocery store clerk for $15 a month, before opening his first store at Lorimer Street & Broadway. He married his wife the day after the store opened — above which they lived.

In the year 1903, the corner of Covert street and Broadway was one of the completely equipped modern stores which were marketed as Brooklyn's quality grocer, and advertised coffee often:

"Paramount Blend Coffee. Perfection in quality; perfect aroma; cannot be equalled anywhere at this price. 5 lbs. for $1.15; per lb. 25¢."

— The Chat, 12 Mar. 1910. (Adjusted for inflation: $36.28 and $7.89 respectively.)

Mr. & Mrs. Bohack became prominent Brooklynites, well known in the 18th and 25th wards. On Friday August 18, 1893, after a fire on Cooper Street (just over the city line at the time) burned 17 houses and rendered 25 families homeless, groceries were provided free of charge by H.C. Bohack. On Thursday November 21, 1901, after Mr. & Mrs. Bohack returned to Brooklyn from an eight month trip to Europe, a surprise party was organized — a full band serenaded the couple outside their home at the corner of Broadway and Putnam, before they were brought to Stanwix Hall, at the corner of Ralph Avenue and Broadway, where a hundred employees and business associates waited with their wives. Dancing was afterward indulged and a genuine German supper was enjoyed.

Bohack Co. continued its successful expansion throughout the city during the first half of the 20th century. While the location at Covert street & Broadway did not last long, the Bohack brand survived until 1977, even being featured in the 66th episode of The Sopranos on HBO.

1893 – 1940s neighborhood clippings

Neighborhood Stories

"BOY HIGHWAYMAN? Patrolman Russel pressed a charge of robbery against Frank Bundy, held for examination to-day. According to Officer Russel, the youngster held up William Eagleston and George Payed, two small boys from Ridgewood, and robbed them of eleven cents and a penknife. Bundy is said to have had two companions who assisted in the robbery."

— Brooklyn Times Union, Jun 25, 1909

During 1909, there was a 12 year old named Frank Bundy living at 3 Covert Street charged with robbery.

Proprietor Mr. J. O'Shaughnessy started H-O Drug Co. in the early 1900s and built it into an institution — "everyone goes there for all kinds of medicine and drug store supplies." They stocked all sorts of medical supplies and novelties, and advertised their delivery automobile as a new and innovative service. Covert St was home to a real estate office, Bushwick Realty Co., during 1923.

1920s – 1960s the building clippings

Flower Market, Fruit Stand, Luncheonette

The address first appears in newspaper advertisements as one of five locations of JAY, PEE, Flower Markets during 1926. Starting around 1928, the corner unit became home of a fruit and vegetable market, with a help wanted listing: "HELP WANTED MALE — Christian young clerk, fruit and vegetable market, salary, Broadway." (The Chat, Saturday June 30, 1928.)

Rental units have always occupied the space above Covert Coffee, while their rent prices have fluctuated dramatically. Units in this building were marketed as having cheap rent, being close to Myrtle Ave, and having a janitor. August — "6 rooms bath, Broadway, apply premises." (Adjusted for inflation equal to $487.12 as of March 2024.) December — "5 rooms, Broadway." (Adjusted for inflation equal to $448.75.)

"LUNCHEONETTE — CUSTARD STORE; corner location; quick sale account of sickness. 1643 Broadway."

— Brooklyn Daily Eagle classified, Jul 01, 1950

In 1957 another grocery store had taken shop, with The Williamsburg News reporting: "Notice is hereby given that License No. 16664 has been issued to the undersigned to sell beer at retail in a grocery store… at 1643 Broadway, Brooklyn, Kings County, N.Y., for off premises consumption."
